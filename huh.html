The world of finance can be a complex and unpredictable place, historical economic crashes are still talked about to this day.

Fear of the next 2008 or worse, the next great depression have been running rife in recent times and in some cases have caused significant changes in governmental policies and actions. The main issue is however, that the centralised banks still have the majority of the say in financial policies and how our money is controlled. Unfortunately errors made by banks are the reason for most significant economic downfalls throughout history.

But what if I told you that there was a solution to the mismanagement of centralised banking and that solution is in the form of digital currency, or it's more common name "cryptocurrency".

Evaluating the use of cryptocurrency to replace centralised banking

Cryptocurrency has been seen as an alternative to centralised currency for over two decades however, only in recent years has it blown up in popularity. Cryptocurrency is an encrypted form of regular currency that can be transferred between two parties without the use of a middleman, cryptocurrencies are notable for their low fees and fast speeds and with thousands of cryptocurrencies available there is likely one that suits you.

ㅤ

ㅤ

The advantages of cryptocurrency:

Low costs and no middleman mean that it is perfect for securely transferring large sums of money without worries of the middleman scamming you.

A uniquely high level of discretion on crypto transactions ensures that people cannot be tracked by those with malicious intent, thus it is also more private than centralised banking.

The largest cryptocurrencies have generally gone up by over hundereds of times their value from just a decade ago, with this trend likely to continue as it becomes more and more popular it is truly just the gift that keeps on giving

ㅤ

ㅤ

The disadvantages of cryptocurrency:

Whilst cryptocurrency generally increases in value overtime, the value of the crypto market is fairly volatile, as it becomes more popular the volatility shoud ease though.

As governments are opposed to decentralisation crypto remains unregulated. This means there is no governmental protection from scams, although they are easy to spot.

So is cryptocurrency really the solution to centralised banks mismanaging our money?

To put it simply, no. There will always be a reason to worry about the next financial crash, and there will always be someone mismanaging our money...
